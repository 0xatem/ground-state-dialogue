# Entities

## What This Section Is

This document examines entities encountered during altered states of consciousness through the framework's lens. It does not explain the entities. It does not categorize them. It presents observations and asks what they mean — or whether meaning is even the right frame.

The framework predicts that the ground state is the foundation of all excitations. If entities are encountered during states in which the perceptual filter is reduced (DMT, extended darkness, deep meditation), they are either: (a) excitations of the ground state with their own structure and agency, (b) the observer's own consciousness encountering itself without the rendering engine's categories, or (c) something the framework cannot account for. The honest answer is that nobody knows which of these is true. The entities themselves, when encountered, do not typically clarify the matter.

## DMT Entity Encounters

### The Hub

DMT sessions consistently begin with a characteristic visual displacement — a sense of being pulled through space at enormous speed — followed by arrival in a realm of pure color and fractal geometry. This space functions as a hub or base layer. It is not a destination. It is the space between destinations. Users report returning to it between locations and re-entering it at the start of subsequent sessions.

The hub is pure color and pattern. No ground, no sky, no objects. Fractals in continuous motion. Not chaotic — structured, self-similar at multiple scales, breathing. This is consistent across a wide range of DMT reports (Strassman, 2001; McKenna, 1983).

### Navigation

Some users report navigating between locations within DMT space with agency — not passively drifting but intentionally moving between spaces using a consistent mechanism. In the account documented here, the experiencer described "riding a zipper" — a felt sense of being transported along a linear track between locations. This was used with intention on multiple occasions within a single session.

### Sound-to-Space Translation

External sound shapes DMT space in real time. In one session, a friend's laugh in the physical room became a wheat field with laughing sunflowers. The auditory input was rendered directly into spatial and entity form without passing through conceptual categories. When the sound changed, the space dissolved.

This is the synesthetic bridge operating at full intensity — not the faint geometry of vowel practice but complete environmental translation. Sound becomes space. Laughter becomes landscape. The rendering engine is translating raw sensory input into visual form without the narrator's categories mediating.

### Entities with Independent Context

Some entities encountered in DMT space appear to have their own context, agenda, and activity independent of the observer. In one encounter, an entity with a bull's head was conducting what appeared to be a business negotiation in an office setting. The entity was alarmed by the observer's presence — the observer was not expected in that space. This implies that DMT space is not constructed entirely by the observer. The entity had a context that preceded the observer's arrival.

### Entities Generated by Sound

Other entities appear to be renderings — translations of physical-world input into DMT-space form. Friends laughing in the physical room appeared as laughing sunflowers. A playful, joyful figure skipping through a field appeared to correlate with external auditory input. These entities may not be independent in the same sense as entities with their own context.

Within a single DMT session, both types can appear: entities that seem to have their own independent activity, and entities that appear to be renderings of physical-world input. The framework does not currently distinguish between these.

## The Dancer: A Repeating Entity

### Description

Across two separate DMT sessions (months apart), the same entity appeared in the same location — an open gymnasium with a grass floor. The entity had a humanoid form but was not human:

- **Six arms**
- **Every individual point on the body radiated the full visible spectrum independently** — not rainbow-colored in the sense of bands of color, but every point simultaneously emitting every frequency of visible light. The effect was of a being made entirely of unfiltered light.
- **Hair braided into perfect spheres** stacking in a pyramid structure — multiple spheres at the base narrowing to a single sphere at the apex. Hair, body, and surface all shared the same light quality. No seams. No boundaries between surfaces. One continuous substance with structure but without separation.
- **Gender unknown.** The entity did not communicate in any language.

No existing religious iconography matches this description. Six-armed deities exist across traditions — Mahakala in Vajrayana Buddhism, Vasudhara, certain forms of Nataraja — but none match these specific features. The rainbow-body tradition in Dzogchen Buddhism (*'ja' lus*) describes bodies dissolving into the five radiant lights of the color spectrum, documented as recently as 1998 (Khenpo A-chos, witnessed by Father Tiso). The dancer's body radiating full spectrum light at every point resembles this more closely than any iconographic tradition, but the dancer was not dissolving — it was a living, watching, active being.

### Behavior

The entity was already present and already watching before the observer became aware of it. It was still while passively observed. When given full, focused, studying attention — not soft gaze but active, direct, locked attention — it began to dance.

The dance was simple. A single fundamental movement. Under sustained focused attention, the entity replicated — copying itself into multiple interlocking versions that formed a dancing mandala. The mandala grew, consuming the observer's field of view. As it expanded, the individual entity became indistinguishable from the pattern. One became many, and the many became one pattern.

Then the mandala dissolved back into the color fractal hub.

The same entity appeared in the same location during both sessions. A random hallucination does not repeat with this specificity across separate sessions.

### What the Dance Communicated

The dance communicated without language — meaning arrived through movement and pattern, understood immediately and without translation:

1. **One becomes many.** Unity became multiplicity through creative action.
2. **Many remain one.** The mandala was not a crowd. It was one pattern at a different scale.
3. **Attention activates creation.** The entity was still until observed with full attention. Observation triggered the creative act — structurally identical to the quantum mechanical observation that the wave function does not collapse into definite form until measured.
4. **The cycle completes.** The mandala dissolved back into the field. Arise, express, dissolve, rest. The breath.

This is a sentence in the [Language of the Birds](language-of-the-birds.md) — meaning communicated without words, through movement and pattern, understood immediately and without translation.

## Traditions on Entities

### What the Framework Can Say

The framework predicts that all manifest reality is excitations of the ground state. If entities encountered in altered states are real — in any sense — they are also excitations of the same field. They are not supernatural. They are natural phenomena occurring at a frequency or dimension that the ordinary rendering engine filters out. DMT removes (or thins) that filter.

This is consistent with:
- The Sufi concept of the *alam al-mithal* (imaginal world) — a realm that is neither purely physical nor purely mental, but ontologically real and accessible through specific practices
- The Tibetan Buddhist concept of the bardo — intermediate states between death and rebirth populated by beings that are projections of mind but are also experienced as objectively real
- The Kabbalistic four worlds (Atziluth, Beriah, Yetzirah, Assiyah) — emanated realms of decreasing density, each populated by beings appropriate to that level
- The Christian angelic hierarchies — beings of pure light organized in structured orders
- Indigenous traditions across the Americas, Australia, and Africa that treat the spirit world as adjacent, accessible, and populated

### What the Framework Cannot Say

Whether entities are:
- Independent beings with their own consciousness, history, and will
- Aspects of the observer's own consciousness externalized by the altered state
- Both simultaneously (the ground state experiencing itself through multiple apertures at once)
- Something else entirely that none of these categories capture

Within a single DMT session, some entities appeared to be independent (the bull-headed entity had its own context and was alarmed by the observer's intrusion) and others appeared to be renderings (the laughing sunflowers were clearly translated versions of physical-world people). The honest position: the framework does not resolve this. The entities are real in the sense that they were experienced. They are consistent in the sense that the dancer appeared in both sessions. They are communicative in the sense that the dance conveyed meaning. Beyond that, the framework holds the question open rather than forcing an answer.

### The Mystic and the Schizophrenic

Wilhelm Reich's distinction (as elaborated by Douglas-Klotz, 2003) is relevant here. Both the mystic and the schizophrenic experience the dissolution of ordinary perceptual boundaries. Both may encounter entities, hear voices, see visions. The difference is integration: the mystic integrates the experience into a coherent self and returns to ordinary function enriched. The schizophrenic fragments — the experience shatters the self rather than expanding it.

The hal/maqam distinction (Qushayri, 11th century) provides structure: a *hal* (state) is a temporary, gifted experience — the vision, the entity, the dissolution. A *maqam* (station) is a permanent, integrated capacity that becomes part of everyday life. An entity encounter is a hal. If the experiencer returns, carries the imprint, and is deepened rather than fragmented by it — that is the mystic pattern, not the schizophrenic one.

The danger is not in the encounter itself. It is in the compulsive pursuit of the encounter — what Maslow called "the polarizing mystic" who needs stronger and stronger triggers. The goal is not to return to the gymnasium. The goal is to build the station that the gymnasium's hal opened up.

## Open Questions

1. **Can entity spaces be accessed without DMT?** Extended darkness (72+ hours), holotropic breathwork, and sustained contemplative practice are the most promising candidates. These remain largely untested in controlled settings.
2. **What are repeating entities?** Entities that appear consistently across sessions — same form, same location, same behavior — do not fit the random hallucination model. Are they stable features of the observer's relationship with the ground state? Independent beings? Archetypes with consistent addresses?
3. **Are entities consistent across observers?** Do different people encounter the same spaces, the same navigation mechanisms, the same types of entities? DMT research (Strassman, McKenna, modern clinical trials) suggests broad structural similarities (the hub, the geometry, entity encounters) but significant individual variation in specific content.
4. **What is the relationship between sound and space?** If external sound shapes DMT space in real time, then auditory practices — the dawn chorus, vowel vibrations, glossolalia (see [language-of-the-birds.md](language-of-the-birds.md)) — may be more directly relevant to accessing entity space than visual practices.
5. **Why do some entities wait?** The dancer was present and watching before the observer arrived. Both times. Same place. Already there. This is the question that does not have even a speculative answer.
